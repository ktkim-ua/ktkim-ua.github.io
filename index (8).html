About

Dr. Kim is a Professor and Chair in the Department of Consumer Sciences at the University of Alabama, where he leads the department and teaches in the CFP Board–registered undergraduate and master's programs. He earned a BA in Economics and a PhD in Consumer Sciences from The Ohio State University, and an MS in Economics from Purdue University. His research is rooted in consumer financial decision-making across three areas: household financial decisions over the life course, retirement preparedness and saving, and the effects of financial literacy and financial education on household outcomes — including current work on financial fraud victimization, AI-based financial advice, and speculative investment behavior. He has published over 80 peer-reviewed articles, cited more than 2,500 times, in journals including the Journal of Consumer Affairs, Journal of Financial Research, International Journal of Bank Marketing, and Journal of Financial Counseling and Planning.
